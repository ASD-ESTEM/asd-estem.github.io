What Is It?

A Student-Led Movement
for STEM in Anglophone East

The ASD-E Student STEM Council is a district-wide collective that unites STEM-passionate students to co-design initiatives, spread helpful programs, and expand real opportunities across schools — supported by mentors, local companies, and the district.

🎙️

Student Voice

A formal platform for students to influence district STEM strategy, priorities, and resource decisions.

⚡

Spark Initiatives

Students lead programs, clubs, and projects that connect peers across schools and grow interest in STEM fields.

🏭

Industry Links

Exposure to local STEM ecosystems — advanced manufacturing, technology, aviation, and sustainable energy.

🌍

Community Impact

Generate real community impact while building skills that align with the North Bay region's workforce needs.

This council is built by students, for students — with mentors, local companies, and the district providing the support needed to make ideas real.

We believe every student in ASD-E deserves access to meaningful STEM experiences — from robotics to aerospace, coding to civil design, and everything in between.

Activities

How We Engage

Council members participate individually and collaborate on district-wide events that bring the whole community together.

🤖

Start or Expand STEM Clubs

Launch and grow robotics, coding, electronics, hacking, and engineering design clubs within your school.

🧑‍🏫

Peer Tutoring & Mentorship

Run peer tutoring or mentorship programs in STEM subjects, helping classmates level up.

🏆

Robotics & Hackathon Teams

Create and lead robotics or hackathon teams, competing locally and representing your school.

👶

Tech Mentors for Younger Grades

Assist as student tech mentors for younger grades or community programs, sharing your skills.

💡

Independent STEM Projects

Develop independent STEM projects — apps, builds, research, competitions, or non-profits.

📢

Outreach & Marketing

Support outreach, marketing, or workshop delivery for district STEM events and initiatives.

🤖 Flagship Event

Robotics Terrain Challenge

A large-scale robotics event where teams design robots to navigate different terrains — aerial, land, maze, obstacles — and participate in races or battles, introducing engineering design and teamwork.

🏢 Career Exposure

Company Connection Day

A rotating workshop event where students move between sessions run by multiple companies and organizations, gaining real exposure to careers, tools, and pathways in STEM industries.

💻 Innovation

District Hackathon

Students collaborate to solve real problems through coding, electronics, design, or data — supported by mentors and judges from local industry and post-secondary partners.

🎉 Celebration

STEM Spirit / STEM Spark Day

A district-wide STEM celebration with breakout rooms students choose from — robotics, coding, electronics, civil design, chemistry, aerospace, art & tech, and much more.

🏕️ Community

STEM Kids Camps

Older students lead hands-on camps or workshops for younger students, building leadership skills while inspiring early interest in STEM fields across the district.

Benefits

Who Gains — And How

The council creates value for students and the broader community simultaneously — building skills while strengthening the STEM ecosystem across New Brunswick.

🎓 Students Gain
  • Leadership and collaboration experience
  • Resume-building STEM involvement and credentials
  • Connections to local industry and post-secondary partners
  • Exposure to real projects and problem solving
  • Opportunities to mentor younger students and be mentored
  • Access to resources, tools, and funding opportunities
🏘️ Community Gains
  • Sustainable student-driven STEM engagement
  • Stronger alignment between student interests and district STEM offerings
  • Enhanced equity and access across all schools
  • Increased visibility for district innovation and student leadership
  • Connections to ICAMP, aviation tech, and sustainable energy programming
  • Growing traction of STEM resources across New Brunswick
  • Impactful student-led initiatives with less strain on teachers
